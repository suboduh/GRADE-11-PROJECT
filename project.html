Vera C. Rubin Observatory

Surveying the Sky for a Better Tomorrow

About the Project

The Vera C. Rubin Observatory is currently being built in Chile. Its main goal is to conduct a 10 year survey of the sky called the LSST (Legacy Survey of Space and Time). This will help us understand things like Dark Matter and Dark Energy.

The Camera

It has a 3.2 gigapixel sensor. That is the biggest digital camera in the world!

The Mirror

The main mirror is 8.4 meters wide, allowing it to see very faint objects in space.

The Data

It will take 200,000 photos per year, creating a massive database for scientists.
